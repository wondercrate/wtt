  • All meals for the duration of the program
  • All sightseeing and visits as noted in the itinerary

Enrollment and Application Process

The following information is required: In accordance with the Transportation Security Administration’s (TSA) Secure Flight program, for all international tours, passport information is required, including the following: full name as it appears on the participant’s passport, date of birth, and gender (for further information, please visit www.tsa.gov. For programs that include round-trip international airfare: If you provide incorrect information to the ticketing agent, or if the participant uses a different passport than originally cited, you will be responsible for charges associated with the ticket’s reissue.

Payments

A deposit of $250 per person, is required to reserve a space on a program. All payments may be made by credit card or check to the program operator, Walking Tree Travel. Deposits are applied to final payment of the program price. The deposit is nonrefundable 72 hours after submission. If the program does not meet the minimum pre-established enrollment, the deposit will be refunded.Final payment is due 75 days prior to departure, unless otherwise noted, upon invoicing. If full payment is not received by the final payment due date, the participant’s place cannot be guaranteed on the program. If an application is received within 90 days of the program departure date, 100% of the program fee will be due at the time of enrollment. Any participant who returns from a program before the scheduled return date forfeits the program fee and must cover all expenses associated with an early return. Financial Aid for participants applying for financial aid, the deposit is 100% refundable if Walking Tree Travel is unable to come to an agreement with the participant’s family on financial aid. When the aid package is finalized, the deposit check will be cashed. The deposit is non-refundable.

Credit Cards

Fees for credit card payments will not be refunded.

Alumni Discount

For open enrollment programs only, a 10% discount will be awarded to all returning alumni and a 30% discount for all Junior Leaders. Alumni status to be verified and approved by Walking Tree Travel at time of enrollment.

Late Registration

Any registration received within 90 days of departure is considered “on-request” and is subject to specific confirmation. Some programs are more restrictive. Payment in full must be made immediately by credit card, or by check sent by overnight mail to Walking Tree Travel, before confirmation is possible. Once confirmed, cancellation penalties apply as set forth below. If confirmation is not possible, all payments made will be refunded in full. Late registration confirmation procedures apply to any participant who is transferred from the wait list to the program within the designated late registration period for that program.

A Note About Costs

All prices are quoted in U.S. dollars. Program costs are based upon current airfares, tariffs, currency values, and on the basis of a presumptive minimum number of participants. While we will do everything possible to maintain the listed prices, they are subject to change. Due to fluctuations in oil prices, a fuel surcharge may be added to the participant’s program fee (e.g. internal flights, cruises, etc.). Details and costs will be advised prior to departure.

Wait List

If the program of the participant’s choice is fully subscribed, you may choose to be placed on a wait list. Individuals on the wait list will be contacted if space becomes available and can inquire about program status by contacting Walking Tree Travel. If you choose to withdraw from a wait list, please notify us.

Cancellation and Refund Policy

Following enrollment, a participant’s withdrawal from the program for any reason, including, but not limited to, illness, injury, family engagements, work-related issues, or geopolitical concerns, etc., will be deemed a cancellation. Please understand that there will be no exceptions to our cancellation policy. Per person cancellation fees are based on date of written notice to Walking Tree Travel. Walking Tree Travel will refund on the following basis:
  • Cancellations within 72 hours of booking: Full Refund
  • Cancellations 120 days prior to departure or less: 75% refund of program cost
  • Cancellations 90 days prior to departure or less: 50% refund of program cost
  • Cancellations 60 days prior to departure or less: No refund
  • Cancellation by WTT : Full Refund or Credit
If the participant has scheduled their own airfare and if the flight is delayed or if for any reason the participant is prevented from joining the program on the scheduled departure date and time, or if the participant chooses to leave the program early, we cannot provide a full or partial refund or credit toward a future program. We reserve the right to cancel any program due to low enrollment or when a U.S. Department of State Travel Warning is issued for the destination.

Air Service

For programs that include round-trip international airfare, rates are based on economy class from designated departure cities as shown. Airfares from other cities are available upon request. Airline upgrades at additional cost may be available on request for the international portion of the participant’s flight. If an air schedule requires an overnight stay in a gateway city, the cost of the overnight will be at the participant’s expense. For programs that include round-trip international airfare, we will be happy to help with arrangements.

Air Service/Tickets Purchased Independently of the Program

International and domestic air schedules are subject to change at any time. If you make the participant’s airline reservations, we recommend that you purchase refundable tickets or tickets with low penalties for changes. We urge you to check airline cancellation penalties before purchasing airline tickets since international departure times and flights can change. Neither the Smithsonian Institution nor Walking Tree Travel accepts liability for cancellation penalties related to domestic or international airline tickets purchased independently in conjunction with a program.

Physical and Medical Considerations

To enjoy the travel experience, participants should be in good physical and mental health. All programs require an element of walking (up to two miles per day over uneven terrain) and of climbing stairs, often without handrails. Participants should have sufficient stamina to keep pace with an active group of participants on long days of programming. If you have any questions about the participant’s ability to participate in a program, please call us at 303-997-0351. Any physical condition, diet, allergy, medication or special request for accommodation must be identified in writing on the Medical Disclosure Form when the enrollment form is completed. Some programs have additional information regarding physical and medical considerations, which may be discussed with Walking Tree Travel at 303-997-0351 or info@smithsonianSA.com. Please be aware that health care standards, facilities, and services abroad may be different or even inadequate for treating certain health conditions. It is essential that you inform us in writing (on the provided Medical Release Form) of any existing condition for which the participant may require medical attention or accommodation during the program. We will make reasonable efforts to accommodate the needs of our participants; however, services of a personal nature cannot be expected or guaranteed. By forwarding the deposit for pre-registration or registration in full, you affirm that the participant does not have any physical or other limitations that would create a hazard for themselves or other participants or would require adjustments to the program for other participants.

Itinerary or Program Modifications

WTT strives to improve program itineraries and their features. If improvements can be made, or unforeseen circumstances beyond our control make changes necessary, at our sole discretion, we reserve the right to vary itineraries and to substitute elements of the program. These elements include, but are not limited to: hotels, staff, speakers, site visits, and services for any stated in the itinerary. Every effort will be made to carry out the program as planned but alterations may still occur after the final itinerary has been sent to you. Please note that weather and other conditions may force changes to the advertised schedule on programs that include water elements. A ship’s captain has the authority to change the ship’s schedule.

Accommodations

Walking Tree Travel chooses lodging based on a combination of safety, cleanliness, location, service, suitability for student groups, and price. Program pricing is based on participants sharing rooms, separated by gender. Detailed information of all program lodging can be found on the program webpage. Some programs involve home stays (accommodations are provided by a family in the community where the program itinerary takes place). Walking Tree Travel works with families in well-vetted home stay communities. Home stay families electing to participate in this program must complete a screening and training process. Where possible, home stay families pass a federal background check and where not possible pass the next closest equivalent. Home stay families also meet with Walking Tree Travel staff for training and orientation where we review our standards and give families guidelines for the hosting experience. Home stays are selected to offer a safe and stable environment with facilities reflective of the local conditions. More information about the community your participant will be a guest in can be found on the program webpage.

Dispute Resolution

If a complaint arises, the participant should first inform a member of the on-program staff, so that the staff can attempt to rectify the matter. If the issue remains unresolved, the individual should submit the complaint in writing to Walking Tree Travel within 30 days of the completion of the program. Any dispute that arises under the program between you and Walking Tree Travel (WTT) (excluding the Smithsonian) that is not resolved through the above process shall be initially submitted to mediation in Denver, Colorado before a mediator agreed upon by you and WTT; if the parties cannot agree upon a mediator, then they shall submit their dispute to the Judicial Arbiter Group, or, if that company no longer exists, to the American Arbitration Association, for mediation. If a dispute is not resolved within thirty days of the holding of a mediation session, then it shall be submitted for binding arbitration to the Judicial Arbiter Group, or, if that company no longer exists, to the American Arbitration Association in Denver, Colorado pursuant to its Commercial Arbitration Rules, except that: (a) disputes concerning $15,000 or less shall be resolved in the small claims or county courts of Denver, Colorado; and (b) disputes in which a party seeks injunctive relief shall be resolved in the courts with jurisdiction over Denver, Colorado, in which case all claims between you and WTT shall be resolved in such lawsuit. You consent and WTT consent to personal jurisdiction and venue being proper in such courts. In an arbitration, the arbitrator’s award shall be final and binding on us and WTT and may be entered in any court having jurisdiction thereof. Attorneys’ fees and costs shall be awarded to the prevailing party. Any action or legal proceeding against the Smithsonian Institution in connection with the program and the Responsibility Statement and Liability Disclaimer shall be brought only in a United States federal court of competent jurisdiction. Standards of Behavior

We aim to provide significant, impactful and fun summer programs for high school students. In order to achieve this goal, program staff and participants must create an environment of trust, security and respect. All individuals participating in WTT programs are required to take responsibility for their actions and adhere to a high standard of behavior. We reserve the right, without penalty, to require any participant to withdraw from the program when their actions are determined by program staff to be in the best interests of the health, safety or general welfare of the program group or the individual participant.

Grounds for Immediate Dismissal Include:

  • Possessing, consuming or distributing alcohol, illegal drugs or tobacco 
  • Associating with participants while they are in possession of or are consuming or distributing alcohol or illegal drugs 
  • Seriously jeopardizing personal or group safety. 
  • Getting a piercing or tattoo
  • Stealing, or deliberately damaging or defacing any personal property, buildings or materials
  • Riding mopeds, motorcycles, or any other type of unauthorized vehicle 
  • Being out of designated area or accommodations after curfew
  • Breaking group rules or disobeying group leaders
  • Consistently displaying a negative attitude, intimidating or excluding other participants, or any other behavior that is not conducive to an atmosphere of trust and mutual respect.
These standards are essential for the successful completion of a WTT program. If a participant breaks any of these standards of behavior, or acts in a way that is harmful to the operation of the program, he/she may be dismissed and sent home early at his/her own expense. In-country consequences are punitive actions determined by the program staff, such as not being able to participate in a community celebration or join the group on a zipline adventure.

Early Return and Cancellation Agreement

In the event that a participant is required to return home from a program for any reason (disciplinary, medical, etc.), other than trip cancellation by us, the parents or legal guardians of the participant are responsible for all costs incurred. In such a circumstance we will do everything we can to keep the costs as reasonable as possible. An itemized invoice will be submitted upon request.

Additional Monies Agreement

We encourage participants to bring ample spending money in the form of cash and a back-up bank-issued debit card (gift cards cannot be used in most countries). In the event that participants must borrow funds from a program leader during the program, Walking Tree Travel must be repaid within 15 days of the program return date.
